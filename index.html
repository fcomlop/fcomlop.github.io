I am currently doing a PhD in Theoretical Neuroscience at the Frankfurt Institute for Advanced Studies, working with Prof. Dr. Jochen Triesch on topics related to Developmental Artificial intelligence. My main research goal is to understand how infants can autonomously develop sensorimotor control and coordination, using a combination of methods such as intrinsically-motivated reinforcement learning, unsupervised reprensentations, and hierarchical architectures. I have also developed several open-source research platforms: MIMo, BabyBench, and OpenEyeSim. Check out my publications and projects to learn more.
